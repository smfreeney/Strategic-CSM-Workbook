Strategic CSM philosophy
Customers don't expect perfection. They expect honesty, empathy, and accountability. Strategic CSMs bake proactive curiosity into every interaction — they don't wait for renewal to uncover risk.
The goal is always to make your customers the hero of their own story — outcome-driven, not product-centric. Every framework here serves that purpose.

Meeting frameworks

Tools for running high-leverage, outcome-focused customer conversations

ACE — meeting opener▼
A
Appreciate
Open with a genuine, specific acknowledgment. Research recent news, personal milestones, or progress. Rehearsed appreciation falls flat — make it real.
C
Confirm
State the meeting goal in under 20 seconds, framed around client outcomes. Present the agenda and invite additions. Must pass the "so what?" test.
E
Engage
"What else would you like to make sure we cover today?" Then stay silent for at least 7 seconds. Respond to additions by incorporating or scheduling separately.
OARS — conversation technique▼
Target a 7:1 ratio of open to closed questions. One question at a time, then silence.
O
Open questions
Start with What, How, Tell me about. Impact: "What's the cost if this isn't addressed?" Value: "If we solved this, what else could you get done?"
A
Affirmations
Specific and genuine. Use at conversation starts and pivotal moments. "The way you've championed this initiative shows real leadership."
R
Reflections
Starters: "It seems like..." / "What I'm hearing is..." Use mirroring (repeat last 3–5 words as a question). Statement tone, not questioning.
S
Summaries
Mandatory at end of every meeting. Weave facts, concerns, opportunities, and emotions. Seek confirmation: "Did I miss anything?"
SOON funnel — discussion to action▼
S
Success
"What would success look like?" Elevates from tactical to strategic. Sets direction for everything that follows.
O
Obstacles
"What's stopping you?" Distinguish symptoms from root causes. Is the client their own obstacle?
O
Options
Good-better-best structure. Lead with "better." Social proof + "What if" questions unlock thinking.
N
Next steps
Importance (0–10) + Confidence (0–10). High: "Why not lower?" Low: "What would move it higher?"
Motivation matrix
High importance / High confidence
Move to specific, time-bound next steps.
High importance / Low confidence
Build capabilities. Break into smaller actions.
Low importance / High confidence
Revisit Success. Use double-sided reflections.
Low importance / Low confidence
Return to earlier SOON stages. One small win.

Discovery

Frameworks for uncovering what customers actually need

Bob London's radically authentic discovery questions▼
ABOUT THEIR BUSINESS
Strategic priority
"What do you think is the biggest priority or challenge your board is discussing?"
Critical outcome
"Out of your entire job description, what's the one thing your company is counting on you to get done?"
90-day whiteboard
"What absolutely has to be off your whiteboard in the next 90 days?"
ABOUT THEM PERSONALLY
Magic wand
"If I gave you a magic wand, what problem would you make disappear first?"
Hardest part
"What's the hardest part of your job?"
Boss's lens
"If your boss knew you were talking to me, what would they want you to tell me or ask me?"
ABOUT YOUR PRODUCT
Switching pain
"On a scale of 1–5, from no-sweat to excruciating, what would it be like to change providers?"
Surprise
"What's one thing that surprised you since you signed the contract?"
Customer for life
"What would make you a customer for life?"

Risk management

Three-phase system — proactive curiosity, smart categorization, swift action

1
Detection (daily)
Traffic light: Green >90% Yellow 50–90% Red <50%

Monitor: usage trends, onboarding progress vs. peers, stakeholder changes, response times, support ticket activity.

"If renewal was tomorrow, from 1–10, how likely would you renew?"
2
Categorization (weekly)
Product gapsProduct issuesLow engagementStakeholder turnoverLow adoptionLow value achievedFinancial instabilityPoor onboardingLow satisfaction
3
Mitigation (when risks confirmed)
Ask for help — colleagues, execs, partners. Scout LinkedIn for internal advocates.

Find root cause — look beyond symptoms (low usage) to underlying problems (low value).
Fill the value gap — additional services, discounts, custom benchmarks.

Paint the future — roadmap aligned to needs, maturity assessments, ROI calculators.

AI prompts for CSMs

PLAN framework prompts — copy, fill in, and use

Prompt 1 — Pre-meeting customer briefing (3Cs)
Purpose: Create a pre-meeting briefing to run a high-leverage conversation with [Customer], and identify the most strategic topic to lead with to achieve [your objective].

Limits: Use up-to-date public sources with citations. Focus on last 90 days. Prioritize signal over volume. Label anything uncertain. Call out risks, tradeoffs, and "what could go wrong."

Audience context: Me: [Your Role] at [Company]. I've supported this customer for [time period]. Meeting with [Contact Name], [title]. They are [time-constrained / ROI-driven / risk-averse / etc.].

Output sections: (1) Core Business Profile (2) Current Strategic Focus & Challenges — last 90 days (3) Stakeholder Profile(s) (4) Actionable insights — single most strategic topic, 2–3 lead lines, 3 proof points
Prompt 2 — Strategic discovery questions
Purpose: Create 8–10 strategic questions to achieve [meeting purpose]. Questions should create productive tension and naturally lead the customer toward the meeting goal.

Limits: Inspired by Bob London's radically authentic discovery approach. Each question must expose a risk, tradeoff, or missed opportunity. Avoid generic questions. Tone: direct, executive, commercially grounded.

Audience: [Customer Name], [Contact Names], [Titles]. Context: [recent interactions, known challenges].

Output: Three buckets — High-level business priorities, Current challenges/pains, Implicit gaps/risks/tradeoffs. Commentary on why to ask each bucket. Ordered for a 5–10 minute conversation.
Prompt 3 — Call assessment
Purpose: You are a call-assessment AI. Evaluate the uploaded transcript using the OARS framework, strategic conversation indicators, depth of discovery, executive presence, and renewal/expansion awareness.

Before assessing: Ask me "What was the meeting goal or desired outcome?"

Output: (A) Summary scorecard 0–10 per category including Open Questions (7:3 ratio target), Affirmations, Reflections, Summary delivered, Strategic influence, Executive presence, Renewal/expansion awareness, Overall score. (B) 3–5 strengths. (C) 3–5 growth opportunities. (D) Suggested phrasing and talk tracks.
PLAN framework
P — Purpose: What you want the AI to do
L — Limits: Sources, length, tone, focus constraints
A — Audience context: Who you are, who the customer is
N — Needed output: Exact sections or format required
